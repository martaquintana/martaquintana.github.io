ABOUT

¡Hello! Get to know me!

My name is Marta Quintana Portales.

I'm a Software Developer with experience in Full Stack development, passionate about technology, art, and nature. Currently, I work part-time at as a Research Assistant at Rey Juan Carlos University, contributing to web development and educational robotics projects.

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Audiovisual Systems and Multimedia Engineering from Rey Juan Carlos University and a Master’s degree in Computer Graphics, Videogames and Virtual Reality from the same institution.


Since I was a child, I have always been fascinated by animated movies. From Borox, a small village located between Madrid and Toledo, Spain, I grew up watching characters come to life on the screen, which sparked a deep interest in the creative process behind them. Although I don't play video games frequently, I also grew up playing on the PS2, Nintendo DS and Wii. Some of my favorite video games were Mario Kart and Pokémon Silver.

In my free time, I enjoy photography, drawing, attending concerts, hiking and cycling. Also I love exploring nature and animals, I love capturing special moments with my camera and exploring the natural beauty on my hikes.

Each of these hobbies allows me to express my creativity in different ways.

I'm open to new Job opportunities where I can contribute, learn and grow.

Master's Thesis at SEDDI:
Parametric Pattern Editing

This project focused on developing a parametric editing tool that allows creating measurement points and modifying 2D and 3D patterns, as well as solving an optimization problem to preserve garment style between avatars with different anthropomorphic measurements.

The project was developed in SEDDI Author as part of a collaboration between SEDDI and the Rey Juan Carlos University (URJC).

Read more Thesis (PDF)

Bachelor's Thesis:
Gamification of an Educational Robotics Web Platform

    I developed a two interactive games at enhancing the learning experience in educational robotics, in collaboration with Kibotics.
  • Roomba Collects Confetti: A programming game where players control a virtual Roomba to collect confetti, integrating basic robotics concepts.
  • Game of the handkerchief: A digital adaptation of the traditional game, developing a new robot designed to catch objects and to engage students in a fun and educational way.
  • Additionally, I conducted research on an Acoustic Teleoperator that allows for controlling a drone using voice commands. This research aimed to explore the integration of machine learning models in robotics and their potential applications in remote control systems.

Currently available in Kibotics
